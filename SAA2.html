Save An Angel

Responsive Redesign for a Non-Profit Organization Website

Project

Project Overview:

Under the UX/UI Bootcamp for McCombs School of Business at UT Austin, we were given a design project in our team worked with a stakeholder (Save An Angel) to help them redesign their Non-Profit Organization's website, in hopes to attract donors, volunteers, and fosters.



Duration:

3 weeks



Project Type:

Research, UI Design (Responsive)



Tools Used:

Adobe XD, Google Suite, Trello, Miro, Slack



Teammates:

4



Role:

UX Researcher, UX Designer



Problem

Save An Angel is a pet rescue organization that requires the help of community giving and involvement to fuel its mission efforts, such as pet rescue, adoption, fostering, fundraising, and transportation.
However, the avenues for involvement are not all represented clearly and consistently on their website, which makes it less likely for users to help the organization.

Our Goal

We believe redesigning the Save an Angel's website to specifically showcase adoption, donation, and volunteer opportunities, will achieve an increase in overall community awareness, donations for their cause, and volunteers willing to assist in saving the lives of pets in need.



The Process

Empathize

Emphasize Phase


Stakeholder Meeting

At the start of the project, our team met with our stakeholder to get a better understanding of what their needs, goals, and wants were for the redesign.

  • Save An Angel's main market is women, aged roughly 45-55 years, who are tech-savvy enough to find their way around Facebook.
  • Most people access the website through mobile. Save an Angel's current website is outdated, which is not responsive to screen-size. We also noticed a high inconsistency in style, images, and fonts.
  • Save An Angel’s advertisement is most successful when it pulls at the heartstrings. Imagery evoking compassion and empathy pulls the users in.
  • Most all of their funding comes from individual contributions, and they rely on many volunteers to execute their mission.


Define/Analyze

User Research

After noting our assumptions, and analyzing Save An Angel's website for usability, it was time to do research to test our hypothesis. We gathered our user research using 3 different methods:



Survey
Sample size: 83


User Interview
Sample size: 7


User Test
Sample size: 7

Key Findings:

01. The two main technical obstacles for donating/volunteering was the lack of follow-up, and confusing website navigation.

02. Users do not trust NPOs that have confusing, or untrustworthy websites with long and difficult processes or forms.

03. Extremely long form process for volunteering, but there are no information on volunteer duties for users to see.

04. Ways to Give' wording is unclear and inconsistent, as users are only given the option to pay. Amazon wishlist is hidden.



Defining

After transcribing our voice data from our user test, we utilized Affinity Mapping to organize and synthesize all the research to identify themes and understand the users' needs.
At the same time, we conducted a business model canvas to define the current problems users were facing, along with our business goals.



Click to view each analysis





User Insight

Through surveys and user tests, we observed that Save An Angel's current website requires excessive effort for users who are trying to help by donating their time and money for the organization.

Defining the Probelm

How might we redesign Save An Angel’s website to maximize visibility and simplicity of involvement so that users can quickly gather information and complete their goals with minimal effort and expense, ultimately increasing users’ involvement?

Our Goal

Redesign Save An Angel’s website to maximize visibility and simplicity of the paths to involvement so that users can quickly gather information and complete their goals with minimal effort and expense, ultimately increasing user involvements.

Ideation



User Persona:

We gathered up all of our information from our research, and created a User Persona, Nancy. Her goals are to either Donate or Volunteer on her free time, while her painpoints are the processes of getting there.



Other Ideation Methods







User Scenerio / Journey

We created two seperate User journey maps, as there are multiple ways to help throughout the website. To visualize both scenarios, we created a Volunteer Journey map, and a Donation Journey Map.

Our main design opportunity lies when users are entering their information to Save An Angel's website.



User Journey Map: Volunteer Flow

User Journey Map: Donation Flow

Redesign Features

Create a simpler, more recognizable navigation for easier finds.

Provide multiple ways to give, with short payment forms with progress information available, as well as allowing users to exit out of the process.

Provide users with adequate information on how they could help, along with the opportunities that are available.

Design / User Test

Lowfi and Midfi

While creating our Low Fidelity sketches and Midfi wireframes on Adobe XD, we considered two navigation styles to conduct an A/B test to identify which style would be more effortless for the user.




A/B Testing

Results

  • For both Desktop and Mobile, users preferred the dropdown navigation style over the sidebar style.
  • Adoption is one of the primary reasons why users come to the page, so it will need it's own category in the primary navigation.
  • For Mobile, we will exchange the dropdown navigation style to a slide-up navigation style, to keep the rule of thumb. Dropdown will be consistent for secondary navigation.





Style Tile

Using our testing results from the Midfi prototypes, we moved forward with our Hifi prototypes and testing. With our stakeholder's preferences in mind, we established the design language for our Save an Angel Redesign.



Hifi Prototypes





Simple, Dropdown / Slide up navigation

Per user observations, our mobile menu popup was moved to the bottom so that users can easily reach it with their thumbs.

As for the web version, the dropdown navigation was changed to a more universal style. This was due to the fact that the prior secondary navigation took too much space at the top along with the header, and the content was getting hidden.



Effortless gifting options

Users are now provided multiple ways to give, with short payment forms with progress information available, as well as allowing users to exit out of the process.
Additionally, we added extra payment options available to make it easy for Venmo and Paypal users.



Volunteer Information

Users are met with three large photos displaying different volunteer opportunities. Users can now easily choose their option to learn more about that opportunity.
If users wanted to sign up to receive information they can easily do so by submitting their email and are then greeted by a playful thank you.

Future Expansion

  • Finish “Donation Impact” page. Show all the good the donations are contributing too, and give donors individual feedback.
  • Add "Operation Relocation" page. Save An Angel’s animal transport program is an important part of its mission. It deserves its own landing page.
  • Research how to direct more users to Save An Angel’s website through its social media and vise versa, as so much of Save An Angel’s advertisement and interaction with the community is through social media.
  • Add a Shop for merchandise. This will offer another way to raise funds and get the word out about Save an Angel.

The Takeaway

Be prepared for anything and have a backup plan! Our stakeholder became wrapped up in prior commitments, so for a portion of the project, we studied organizations similar to Save An Angel to get a better understanding of non-profit pet rescues.

Going into the project, we thought we knew how adoption processes and how a non-profit is run, but once we interviewed individuals who are in the industry, we realized we were far off. This is where our research phase paid off!

Using Agile PM methodology, my team had the ability to accomplish massive goals by dividing up responsibilities, provide constructive feedback to one another, and execute demanding deadlines.

Overall, I am grateful to have worked with amazing, talented teammates who each brought their own unique skills to the project!
