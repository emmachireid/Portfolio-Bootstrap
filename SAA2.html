Responsive redesign for a Non-Profit Organization Website




Project
Project Overview:
Under the UX/UI Bootcamp for McCombs School of Business at UT Austin, we were given a design project in our team worked with a stakeholder (Save An Angel) to help them redesign their Non-Profit Organization's website, in hopes to attract donors, volunteers, and fosters.


Duration:
4 weeks


Project Type:
UX Research, UI Design (Responsive), Front-end Development


Tools Used:
Adobe XD, Google Suite, Trello, Miro, Slack


Teammates:
4


Role:
UX Designer and Developer








Problem
Donators, volunteers, and fosters have multiple options as to which NPO organizations to give their time and money. We want provide users with processes that require minimal effort, so they would return to the website to offer help again.







The Process




Research

Emphasize Phase


Stakeholder Meeting
At the start of the project, our team met with our stakeholder to get a better understanding of what their needs, goals, and wants were for the redesign.
  • Save An Angel's main market is women, aged roughly 45-55 years, who are tech-savvy enough to find their way around Facebook.
  • Most people access the website through mobile. Save an Angel's current website is outdated, which is not responsive to screen-size. We also noticed a high inconsistency in style, images, and fonts.
  • Save An Angel’s advertisement is most successful when it pulls at the heartstrings. Imagery evoking compassion and empathy pulls the users in.
  • Most all of their funding comes from individual contributions, and they rely on many volunteers to execute their mission.








User Research

After noting our assumptions, and analyzing Dell’s website for usability, it was time to do research to test our hypothesis. We gathered our user research using 3 different methods; surveys, user interviews, and user tests.


Key Findings:

  • The two main technical obstacles for donating/volunteering was the lack of follow-up, and confusing website navigation. (survey)
  • Users do not trust NPOs that have confusing, or untrustworthy websites with long and difficult processes or forms. (interview)
  • Extremely long form process for volunteering, but there are no information on volunteer duties for users to see. (user test)
  • Ways to Give' wording is unclear and inconsistent, as users are only given the option to pay. Amazon wishlist is hidden.(user test)

Our Goal

Redesign Save An Angel’s website to maximize visibility and simplicity of the paths to involvement so that users can quickly gather information and complete their goals with minimal effort and expense, ultimately increasing users’ involvements.

A/B Testing

Results

  • For both Desktop and Mobile, users preferred the dropdown navigation style over the sidebar style.
  • Adoption is one of the primary reasons why users come to the page (although it is our goal for this specific redesign), so it will need it's own category in the primary navigation.
  • For Mobile, we will exchange the dropdown navigation style to a slide-up navigation style, to keep the rule of thumb. Dropdown will be consistent for secondary navigation.






Style Tile

Using our testing results from the Midfi prototypes, we moved forward with our Hifi prototypes and testing. With our stakeholder's preferences in mind, we established the design language for our Save an Angel Redesign.









Hifi Prototypes

Hifi-Prototyping

As mentioned in the redesign feature, we focused on condensing certain information that felt overwhelming to read, such as the adoption processes and volunteer information. With our redesign, users are not obligated to read or scroll through tedious, long blocks of text, and instead are able to click or expand to learn more about these specific areas.

For the donation process, users will now be able to choose their ways to help. Payment forms are short with progress information available. Additionally, we made it easier for users to exit out of this donation process if for some reason they felt uncomfortable during the process.

To have our users empathize with the cause, we incorporated more visuals to our redesign. Additionally, we added the company's mission statement on the Home Page so that users are introduced to the company and their cause at their first glance.
