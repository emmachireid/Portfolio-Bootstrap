Save An Angel


Responsive redesign for a Non-Profit Organization Website

Under the UX/UI Bootcamp for McCombs School of Business at UT Austin, we were given a design project in our team worked with a stakeholder (Save An Angel) to help them redesign their Non-Profit Organization's website, in hopes to attract donors, volunteers, and fosters.




Project Overview:

Duration: 4 weeks
Teammates: 4
Project Type: UX Research, UI Design (Responsive)


Role

Project Manager, Lead designer


Tools Used:

Adobe XD, Google Suite, Trello, Miro, Slack



Problem

Through surveys and user tests, we observed that Save An Angel's current website requires excessive effort for users who are trying to help by donating their time and money for the organization.

Solution

Redesign Save An Angel’s website to maximize visibility and simplicity of the paths to involvement so that users can quickly gather information and complete their goals with minimal effort and expense, ultimately increasing users’ involvements.





Research



Stakeholder Meeting


  • Save An Angel's main market is women, aged roughly 45-55 years, who are tech-savvy enough to find their way around Facebook.

  • Most people access the website through mobile. Save an Angel's current website is outdated, which is not responsive to screen-size. We also noticed a high inconsistency in style, images, and fonts.

  • Save An Angel’s advertisement is most successful when it pulls at the heartstrings. Imagery evoking compassion and empathy pulls the users in.

  • Most all of their funding comes from individual contributions, and they rely on many volunteers to execute their mission.




User Research

After noting our assumptions, and analyzing Save An Angel's website for usability, it was time to do research to test our hypothesis.
We gathered our user research using 3 different methods:

Surveys


Gather quantitative data on why people choose to volunteer or to give, and what they find as a pain during any of the process.







Summary:


  • The two main technical obstacles for donating/volunteering was the lack of follow-up, and confusing website navigation.

  • 2. While Donation opportunities were found mostly on social media, people rely on Web Search when finding out about adoption.



User Interviews


Gather general data and insights from people who volunteer to understand what users would find important on a NPO website.







Summary:


  • Users were motivated to donate/volunteer when they felt close to the cause, and when they knew what they were getting into.

  • Users do not trust NPOs that have confusing, or untrustworthy websites with long and difficult processes.



User Tests


Gather quantitative data on people's thoughts while navigating through the current website, pinpointing the opportunities .







Summary:


  • Extremely long form process for volunteering, but there are no information on volunteer duties for users to see.

  • 'Ways to Give' wording is unclear and inconsistent, as users are only given the option to pay. Amazon wishlist is hidden.



Afterwards, I Transcribed our notes and conducted Affinity Mapping, SWOT analysis, and created a Business Model Canvas.

Final Prototype

As mentioned in the redesign feature, I focused on condensing certain information that felt overwhelming to read, such as the adoption processes and volunteer information. Now users will be able to click or expand to learn more about these specific areas.

For the donation process, users will now be able to choose their ways to help. Payment forms are short with progress information available. Additionally, I made it easier for users to exit out of this donation process if for some reason they felt uncomfortable during the process.

To have our users empathize with the cause, I incorporated more images to our redesign. Additionally, I added the company's mission statement on the Home Page so that users are introduced to the company and their cause at their first glance.
