Overview

Within a three week time span. Group of 4 teammates. As a project manager, my task was to make sure all the deliverables were ready within the short time frame we were given. For this team, we discussed and decided NOT to give specific roles to each member. This is because we wanted to test out everything ourselves, and discuss everything together as a team. While we may have taken a longer way, we were able to individually figure out which tasks they had an advantage on.

The Problem

Save An Angel is a pet rescue organization that requires the help of the community to fuel its mission efforts. There is a high interest in getting involved in different areas of the animal rescue mission, including volunteering, adopting, fostering, donating and supporting fundraising events, but users are less likely to help as the required effort increases or resource availability decreases. Therefore, users need a way to give back with minimal effort and roadblocks.


The Research

1. Stakeholder Meeting

Save An Angel's main market is women, aged roughly 45-55 years, who are tech-savvy enough to find their way around Facebook.

Most people access the website through mobile. Save an Angel's current website is outdated, which is not responsive to screensize. We also noticed a high inconsistency in style, images, and fonts.

Save An Angel’s advertisement is most successful when it pulls at the heartstrings. Imagery evoking compassion and empathy pulls the users in.

Most all of their funding comes from individual contributions, and they rely on many volunteers to execute their mission.


2. Survey Results

  • 91% of people had interest in volunteering
  • 84% of people find out about opportunities to give through social media
  • 85% of people think it is important to see the difference their donation is making

3. Usability Testing Results


Before we conducted user interviews, we ran a couple usability test to see what the current website's actual usability was like.

  • Donation is crucial to the website, yet the wording is inconsistent: [“Donate” vs. “Ways to Give”]
  • Volunteer page offers no information on volunteer duties, and only included an extremely long form, which users said is a deterrent.
  • To donate and to see available dogs, the site takes you to a third party, which is not appealing to users.
  • Add more information on and feedback for donating. Amazon Wishlist needed to be better advertised.

4. Interviews

After creating a script for user interviews, our team members individually proceeded to conduct user interviews.
We focused on asking questions about their experiences and connections with donation and volunteer opportunities.


I had our teammates write in their version of transcription into the google sheets that I had created. This way, we were able to make sure that we asked the same questions, and the answers were more easy to compare.





Once we finished writing in our transcriptions for comparison, we also created an Affinity Diagram.
By doing this, we were able to see clearly that our users had common desires and painpoints about Volunteering and Donating.
