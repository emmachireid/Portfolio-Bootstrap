Thrill


UI Design Challenge from Goodbrief.io

Challenge description: You will be creating a website for a real-estate company named "Thrill". They provide co-working spaces for individuals, and the website's main target is to sell the company's products.





Project Overview


Deadline: 3 days
Project Type: UI Design (Web)


Design Requisite

1. Create a Landing Page, Contact Page, Shop Page, Terms of Service Page.
2. Landing page should maximize conversions, and should have "Our Values" section.
3. There should be a CTA to get users to follow the company's social media.
4. Brand Color: Black. Design should be luxurious, yet have a modern feel.


Tools Used

Adobe XD



Low-fi Sketches

To the left are cleaned up images for my Low-fi Prototype. This was definitely where I spent the most time - luxurious designs are not something I am used to. The first couple of designs that I drew (and designed loosely on XD) all turned out looking old-fashioned, and out of date.
I spent time doing my research and collecting ideas on websites like dribbble, pinterest, and other mediums. I even went to a co-working cafe in my city to grasp an idea of what it actually looked like inside.




Mid-fi



Once I was happy with my idea of luxurious and modern website, I cleaned up the sketches I had made on XD and turned them into my Mid-fi sketch. I wanted the website to have bold, raw images throughout the website to give my user an idea of what the place would actually look like, without having to book a tour first (which is what a lot of co-working real estates do, as I learned through my research).
I decided on fonts style at this point since I was running out of time and did not have the bandwidth to change this during my Hi-fi.




Style Tile:


Once the Midfi-Protytpe was completed, I created the style tile to keep a consistent theme throughout the website. I focused on keeping my colors mostly neutral and dark for the luxurious effect.


Not mentioned in Style Tile:


  • Dark Overlay over images with text for accessibility

  • Wasted time working on a page that was not in the requisite (purchase flow), due to misunderstanding
