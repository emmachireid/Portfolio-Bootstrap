UI Design Challenge for Plant E-commerce website





Project
Overview:
Plantisa is a website for online house-plant shopping. Personal challenge to create a purchase userflow for an indoor plant-selling e-commerce website under 2 days.

Goal:
Create a friendly and appealing website where users would feel safe and encouraged to purchase a plant.

Duration:
2 days

Project Type:
UI Design (Web)

Tools Used:
Adobe XD










Lowfi Sketches
Things that always comes first: Pencil and a paper. I designed my low-fis here, and cleaned them up. I created a list of pages to draw first, however nearly 1/3 of those pages were never created, due to priority.

While I was satisfied with what I had imagined, the sketches looked less appealing on paper. I added notes of how I wanted certain components to act to test in my Mid-fi.










Midfi Sketches
Once the Low-fi sketches were in Adobe XD as Mid-fi prototype, I realized that the corners seemed harsh. Other than that, I was pretty happy with my process - so I went on ahead to create hi-fi prototype from here.

Since I had already decided on font-size and weight prior to making the style tile, it was easier to see the hierarchy that I had imagined in my Low-fi sketches.









Style Tile
Once the Midfi-Protytpe was completed, I created the style tile to keep a consistent theme throughout the website. I used a light blue color to establish user's sense of security, while still giving off a friendly feel.

Lowercase for certain areas:
Creates an approachable atmosphere at first sight.

Dark Overlay over certain images:
Simply for accessibility, as most of my images were on the lighter side.











Hi-fi Prototypes
After finishing the style tile, it was time for me to create the Hi-fi. Since I did not end up changing too much from my Mid-fi Prototypes, this was fairly an easy task (other than changing the border for all the boxes in place).











Opportunities
Although I was able to ultimately meet my goal, there are certain pages that were not made into this prototype, solely due to that fact that the time duration of this challenge did not allow me to create pages that were lower in priority. This includes account pages, search results page, terms/conditions page, 404 pages, and so on.

Another opportunity I had missed was allowing users to have an option to checkout as prior users (has an account). My current prototype only allows users to checkout as guests, which forces users to enter their Contact, Address, and Payment information again, even if they are a returning user.
